What the charts tell us
📊 Chart 1 takeaway

The top 4 causes alone account for over 1.5 million US deaths annually — roughly 55% of all deaths. Everything else is noise by comparison.

📊 Chart 2 takeaway

The log scale reveals the true absurdity: a shark killing you is roughly 600,000× less likely than heart disease. Your brain cannot intuitively process this gap.

📊 Chart 3 takeaway

Media coverage of dramatic deaths (violence, accidents, rare events) inflates our perceived risk dramatically. The availability heuristic at work.

📊 Chart 4 takeaway

Fire ant anaphylaxis (~60/yr) kills more Americans than shark attacks, lightning, and plane crashes combined — and gets approximately zero documentary coverage.
